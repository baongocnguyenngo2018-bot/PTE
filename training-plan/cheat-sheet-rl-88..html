Day 3 Drill

RETELL LECTURE MASTERY

Target 88+: Content Quality + Fluid Transitions

1. Active Listening

Don't write everything. Listen for Signposts ("However", "First", "Finally", "Significantly"). These signal a scoring point is coming.

2. Note-Taking Logic

Write Subject + Verb pairs. Single words are dangerous because you forget the context.
e.g., "Population rose" NOT just "Population"

3. Time Mgmt

You have 40s. Speak for 30-35 seconds. If you speak too little (<20s), you lose Content points. If you ramble >40s, you get cut off.

The "Flow" Template

Memorize this. It forces pauses and intonation automatically.

The speaker was discussing the main topic of [TOPIC / TITLE].

Firstly, he mentioned that [KEY POINT 1 + DETAIL].

He also highlighted that [KEY POINT 2], which is significant because [REASON / DETAIL].

Furthermore, he discussed [KEY POINT 3].

In conclusion, the lecture was very informative regarding [TOPIC].

Intonation Tip: Raise your pitch on the Connector phrases and drop your pitch at the end of the data points.

Note-Taking Blueprint

How to structure your erasable notebook.

TOPIC: Global Warming
  • 1. Temp rise -> fast rate
  • 2. CO2 emissions -> cars/factories
  • 3. Impact -> sea level + ice melt
  • 4. Sol -> green energy
(Write vertically. Use arrows. Ignore grammar.)

Goal: Capture 3-5 distinct phrases.

Bad Note: "Environment... bad... years..." (Too vague)

Good Note: "Environment damaged 50 years" (Subject + Verb + Time)

PANIC MODE

What if I didn't understand anything?

Scenario A: You caught 1-2 words only.

Do not stay silent. Use the "Keyword Loop".

"The speaker was discussing [Word you heard]. He mentioned the importance of [Word]. Furthermore, he discussed the future implications of [Word]. In conclusion, [Word] is very important."

Scenario B: You can't read your own handwriting.

Abandon the note immediately. Do not squint.

  • Skip to the next readable point immediately.
  • Use a filler: "The lecture also provided details about the subsequent factors."
  • Then jump to your next clear note.

Day 3 Drill Protocol (30 Questions)

Use your 30 practice questions to complete this progressive intensity drill.

Set 1: Template Anchor (5 Qs)

Recite the template EMPTY 3 times. Then do 5 questions. Priority: Smooth flow over accurate content.

Set 2: Note-Taking (10 Qs)

Focus on writing "Subject + Verb" notes. Don't worry about speaking perfectly, worry about getting clear, readable notes.

Set 3: Exam Sim (10 Qs)

Strict timing. 10 seconds prep, 40s speak. No stopping. If you stumble, keep moving. Force fluency.

Set 4: Audio Focus (5 Qs)

Close your eyes or turn off the screen. Rely 100% on your ears to capture the keywords. Good for difficult accents.
