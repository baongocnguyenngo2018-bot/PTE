Day 2 Drill

DESCRIBE IMAGE MASTERY

Target 88+: Zero Hesitation • Maximum Speed

Rule #1: 25 Seconds Max

You have 40s, but aim to finish in 25-30 seconds. Dragging it out increases the risk of grammar errors and "umms". Speed is confidence.

Rule #2: The "Also" Trick

If you get stuck, don't stop. Say: "The image also reveals information about..." and read the title again or a random number.

Rule #3: No Analysis

Do not say "because" or try to explain why the data is high. Just report what you see physically. You are a reporter, not an analyst.

1. Data Charts

Bar • Line • Pie • Table

The image gives information about [TITLE].

From the picture, it is clear that [HIGHEST ITEM] has the highest figure, which is [NUMBER].

On the contrary, [LOWEST ITEM] has the lowest figure, which is [NUMBER].

It can also be seen that [RANDOM ITEM] is higher than [RANDOM ITEM].

In conclusion, the trend is increasing/decreasing.

Example: Bar Chart "Fruit Sales 2023"

"The image gives information about Fruit Sales in 2023. From the picture, it is clear that Bananas has the highest figure, which is 500 units. On the contrary, Apples has the lowest figure, which is 100 units. It can also be seen that Oranges is higher than Grapes. In conclusion, the trend is fluctuating."

2. Maps

World • City • Layout

The map provides information about [TITLE/LOCATION].

In the northern part, we can see [CITY/FEATURE A], while in the south, there is [CITY/FEATURE B].

On the west side, [FEATURE C] is visible, whereas on the east side, we have [FEATURE D].

The map also displays various colors such as [COLOR 1] and [COLOR 2].

In conclusion, the map is very informative.

Example: Map of Australia Rainfall

"The map provides information about Annual Rainfall in Australia. In the northern part, we can see Darwin, while in the south, there is Tasmania. On the west side, Perth is visible, whereas on the east side, we have Sydney. The map also displays various colors such as dark blue and light yellow. In conclusion, the map is very informative."

3. Process / Flowcharts

Cycles • Steps • Arrows

The diagram provides information about the process of [TITLE].

In the first stage, we can see [STEP 1].

Following that, the process continues with [STEP 2] and [STEP 3].

Then, it leads to the stage of [STEP 4].

Finally, the process concludes with [FINAL STEP].

In conclusion, this is a complex process with various stages.

Example: The Water Cycle

"The diagram provides information about the process of The Water Cycle. In the first stage, we can see Evaporation from the ocean. Following that, the process continues with Condensation and Cloud Formation. Then, it leads to the stage of Precipitation. Finally, the process concludes with Surface Runoff. In conclusion, this is a complex process with various stages."

4. Photos / Weird Images

Photographs • abstract • No text

The picture provides information about [TITLE / "a beautiful scene"].

In the foreground, I can clearly see [OBJECT/COLOR 1].

However, in the background, there is [OBJECT/COLOR 2].

On the left hand side, [FEATURE A] is visible, and on the right hand side, there is [FEATURE B].

In conclusion, the image highlights the relationship between [A] and [B].

Example: Photo of a Modern Library

"The picture provides information about a modern library interior. In the foreground, I can clearly see wooden tables and students studying. However, in the background, there is a large glass window. On the left hand side, bookshelves are visible, and on the right hand side, there is a staircase. In conclusion, the image highlights the relationship between architecture and learning."

ADVANCED

5. Mixed Charts (e.g., Pie + Line)

Strategy: Treat it as ONE big image. Do not pause to switch between them.

The Bridge Phrase:

"Furthermore, the second chart shows that..."

...[Describe Graph 1 Max/Min]...

Moving to the second chart,

it displays information about [Title 2]. The highest value is [X]...

In conclusion, both charts are significant.

Example: Pie Chart (Sources of Energy) + Line Graph (Usage over Time)

"The image gives information about Energy Sources. From the first picture, it is clear that Coal has the highest figure, which is 40 percent. Moving to the second chart, it displays information about Usage over 10 years. The highest value is in 2020, which is 100 units. In conclusion, both charts are significant."

Day 2 Training Protocol

Set 1: Bar & Line

Practice 10 Bar charts and 10 Line graphs. Focus on identifying "Highest" and "Lowest" in < 3 seconds.

Set 2: The Weird Ones

Practice 5 Maps and 5 Processes. Force yourself to use the "North/South" and "First/Next" templates.

Set 3: Speed Drill

Do 5 random images. You strictly have only 15 seconds prep (simulate hard conditions).

Set 4: Review

Listen to recordings. Did you say "Umm"? Did you pause > 1 second? If yes, repeat.
