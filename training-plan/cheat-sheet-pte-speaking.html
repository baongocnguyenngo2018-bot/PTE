Read Aloud

Skill: Intonation & Stress

Questions 6 - 7
Prep 30 - 40s
Speak (Max) 40s Rec: 30-35s

The Golden Rule

Stress Content Words. Speed up Function Words.

  • Stress: Nouns, Verbs, Adjectives.
  • Glide: The, a, of, to, in, and.

The "Phonemic Bluff" Protocol

Never skip a word. If you see "Biopharmaceutical" and don't know it, say "Bio-pharma-ceutical" with 100% confidence. Hesitation kills scores; wrong pronunciation does not.

Repeat Sentence

Skill: Short Term Memory

Questions 10 - 12
Prep Audio Ends
Speak (Max) 15s Rec: Immediate

The Echo Chamber

Do not translate meaning. Hold the sound.

Input: "The assignment must be submitted by Friday."

79: "The assignment... submitted." (50%) 88: "The assignment... submitted by Friday." (80%)

Panic Mode

"Say ANYTHING you heard."

If you forget the sentence, speak the 2-3 words you remember with perfect intonation. Silence is a zero. A partial sentence is points.

The 40-Second Template

The speaker was discussing the main topic of [TOPIC].

Firstly, he mentioned that [KEY POINT 1 + DETAIL].

He also highlighted that [KEY POINT 2], which is significant because [REASON/DETAIL].

Furthermore, he discussed [KEY POINT 3].

In conclusion, the lecture was very informative regarding [TOPIC].

The Triad Template

The audio presents a discussion regarding [TOPIC].

The first speaker initiated the conversation by stating that [POINT 1].

In response, the second speaker [AGREED/DISAGREED], pointing out that [POINT 2].

Furthermore, the third speaker added a new perspective regarding [POINT 3].

Overall, the group concluded that [OUTCOME/CONSENSUS].

Skill: Reflexes

Questions 5 - 6
Prep Audio Ends

Rule 1: One Word

"What do we use to see stars?"
"Telescope" (Correct)
"We use a telescope" (Wrong)

Rule 2: The "OR" Trick

"Is it X or Y?"
The answer is 90% likely to be either X or Y. Listen for the choices.

Panic: The Echo

If you don't know, repeat the main noun from the question. Better than silence.
