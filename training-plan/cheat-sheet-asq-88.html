Speed Drill

ANSWER SHORT QUESTION

Target 88+: Instant Reflexes + Vocabulary Precision

1. Zero Hesitation

The microphone opens immediately. Do not say "Ummm" or "I think it is...". Just say the word.
Goal: < 1 second delay.

2. The "One Word" Rule

Do not speak full sentences. Use articles (A/The) if you want, but single nouns are best.
Q: "What do we use to see stars?" A: "Telescope" (NOT "We use a telescope")

3. Academic Vocab

Most questions are about University life, Science, or simple logic. If you hear "library", the answer is often "Book" or "Librarian".

The Reflex Protocol

How to handle the 3 types of prompts.

Type A: The Definition

"What do you call a person who flies a plane?"

"Pilot"

Type B: The Visual

(Shows a picture of a circle cut in half)

"Semicircle" / "Half"

Type C: The Choice

"Which is longer, a century or a decade?"

"Century"

Tip: The answer is almost always IN the question.

PANIC MODE

"I Don't Know The Answer"

Strategy A: The Echo

If you have absolutely no idea, repeat the main noun from the question clearly. It's better than silence.

Q: "What is the study of celestial bodies?"
Your brain: ???
You say: "Celestial Bodies." (Might get 0 for logic, but keeps fluency for next task)

Strategy B: The Logical Guess

If asked for a "Document" or "Person", guess the most common ones.

"Document" -> Passport / ID
"Person" -> Student / Professor
"Building" -> Library

Drill Protocol: Rapid Fire

ASQ is about volume. Do 50 questions in a row.

Set 1: YouTube Speed (20 Qs)

Find a "PTE Answer Short Question" compilation on YouTube. Play at 1.25x speed. Force your brain to react faster.

Set 2: Visual ID

Google "Basic 3D Shapes" and "Basic Laboratory Equipment". Name every image you see in under 1 second.

Set 3: The "Or" Trick

Practice listening for questions with "OR".
"Is it X OR Y?"
The answer is 90% likely to be one of those two words.

Set 4: Mute Check

While watching the news or a video, mute it randomly and name the first object you see on screen immediately.
