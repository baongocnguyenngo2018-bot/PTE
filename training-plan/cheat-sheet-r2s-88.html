Core Skill Drill

RESPOND TO A SITUATION

Target 88+: Appropriate Tone + Clear Solution

1. The Tone Switch

Identify the audience immediately. Neighbor/Friend = Casual. Boss/Official = Formal. Using "Sir" with a friend lowers your score.

2. The Goal Arc

Every prompt has a goal (e.g., apologize, complain, suggest). You must explicitly state the solution. Don't just describe the problem.

3. Conciseness (40s)

You have 40 seconds. Aim for 25-30 seconds. It's better to be short, polite, and clear than to ramble and lose the "purpose".

The Dual Template

Choose your path instantly based on the prompt.

Formal Path (Professor, Manager, Police, Elderly)

"Hello [TITLE/NAME],"

"I am calling/speaking to you today regarding [THE ISSUE]."

"Unfortunately, there has been a situation where [DETAILS]."

"Therefore, I would like to suggest that we [SOLUTION]."

"Thank you for your understanding."

Informal Path (Friend, Neighbor, Classmate, Sibling)

"Hi [NAME],"

"Look, I need to talk to you about [THE ISSUE]."

"The thing is, [EXPLAIN PROBLEM SIMPLY]."

"So, why don't we [SOLUTION]?"

"Let me know if that works for you."

  • Yes = "Hi/Hey" + Simple words
  • No/Superior = "Hello" + Polite words
POWER PHRASES

Soft Skills & Emergencies

How to say "NO" (Politely)

Never just say "No". Use the "Sandwich Method".

1. Positive: "I'd really love to help..."
2. Negative: "...but unfortunately I am fully booked that day."
3. Positive: "However, I can help you next week."

Emergency: Brain Freeze

If you can't think of a specific solution, use the Generic Compromise.

Formal: "Could we schedule a meeting to discuss this in more detail?"
Informal: "Why don't we grab a coffee later and figure this out together?"

Drill Protocol: The "Chameleon" Drill

This task tests adaptability.

Drill 1: The Switch

Take 1 scenario (e.g., "Late for a meeting"). Record it twice: Once for your Best Friend, once for your CEO. Listen to the difference.

Drill 2: Apology Master

Apologizing is 50% of questions. Practice variations: "I apologize sincerely" (Formal) vs "I'm so sorry, my bad" (Informal).

Drill 3: The 30s Cap

Don't ramble. Force yourself to finish the entire arc (Greeting -> Issue -> Sol -> Bye) in exactly 30 seconds.

Drill 4: Tone Check

Record yourself. If you sound "monotone" or "robotic", you lose points. Exaggerate your emotion slightly (Concern/Regret/Happiness).
