New Task Drill

SUMMARIZE GROUP DISCUSSION

Target 88+: Speaker Differentiation + Logical Synthesis

1. Identify the Dynamics

It's not a monologue. Who is Agreeing and who is Disagreeing? Capture the conflict or the consensus.

2. The Grid Method

Do not write a list. Split your paper into 3 Columns (Speaker 1, 2, 3). This prevents you from attributing the wrong idea to the wrong person.

3. Endurance (2 Mins)

CRITICAL: You have 2 minutes (120s), not 40s. Speaking for only 40s here will kill your score. Aim for 90+ seconds of detailed summary.

The "Triad" Template

Designed to handle 3 speakers clearly. Memorize the transitions.

The audio presents a discussion between speakers regarding the main topic of [TOPIC / ISSUE].

The first speaker initiated the conversation by stating that [S1 ARGUMENT]. They emphasized that [S1 DETAIL].

In response, the second speaker [AGREED / DISAGREED], pointing out that [S2 KEY POINT].

Furthermore, the third speaker added a new perspective regarding [S3 KEY POINT].

Overall, the group concluded that [OUTCOME / DECISION].

Fluency Tip: Don't rush! You have 120 seconds. Use a slower, more deliberate pace than Retell Lecture. Elaborate on the "Why" for each speaker.

The "Grid" Strategy

Structure your erasable notebook vertically.

TOPIC: Remote Work
S1 (Mgr)
  • - Prod. dropping
  • - Miss deadlines
  • - Need office
S2 (Emp)
  • - Save commute
  • - Better focus
  • - Disagrees
S3 (Sol)
  • - Hybrid model
  • - 3 days home
  • - Compromise
(Draw 2 vertical lines before audio starts. Don't mix them up!)

Goal: Identify the "Role" if possible (e.g., Manager, Student, Professor).

The "Link": Note if S2 supports (+) or opposes (-) S1.

PANIC MODE

The "Generic Speaker" Backup

Scenario A: You lost track of who said what.

Stop using "First/Second Speaker". Switch to "One opinion" and "Another view".

"The discussion covered [TOPIC]. One perspective was that [POINT A]. However, another view presented was that [POINT B]. Additionally, it was mentioned that [POINT C]."

Scenario B: You ran out of content at 40 seconds.

You MUST fill time. Use the "Implication Loop".

  • Don't stop. Add: "This is a crucial point because it impacts the overall outcome of the [TOPIC]."
  • Recap the consensus again: "Therefore, understanding these different viewpoints is essential for solving [TOPIC]."

Drill Protocol: The "Endurance" Test

This task is about stamina. 40s is easy. 2 minutes is hard.

Drill 1: The Divider

Play an audio. Do NOT write words. Just draw a checkmark in Column 1, 2, or 3 every time the speaker changes. Train your ear to detect voice changes.

Drill 2: The 90s Wall

Record yourself summarizing a 3-minute news debate. You are NOT allowed to stop before 90 seconds. If you run out of ideas, paraphrase the intro again.

Drill 3: The "However"

Practice using contrast words. In this task, speakers often disagree. Force yourself to use "Conversely", "On the other hand", and "Disagreed" in every practice.

Drill 4: Name Tagging

Try to give them names in your head (e.g., "The Pessimist", "The Optimist", "The Expert"). It makes summarizing their "vibe" much easier.
